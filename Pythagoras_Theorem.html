Pythagoras Theorem Explained

The Pythagoras Theorem is one of the most fundamental concepts in geometry. It states: In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Formula: c² = a² + b²

This theorem is widely used in trigonometry, coordinate geometry, and even real-world applications like architecture, engineering, and navigation.

In JEE and other competitive exams, it forms the base for multiple topics including circles, triangles, and vectors. Make sure you understand how to apply it, not just remember the formula!

Example: If a triangle has sides a = 3 and b = 4, then the hypotenuse c is √(3² + 4²) = √25 = 5.
